Meet the team

Members of the Loon mechanical engineering team handle prototypes of equipment they’ve built in-house. They are adapting the designs for at-scale production.

Loon electrical engineers own their designs from concept all the way to field deployment.

Chris stands beside two fully assembled sets of Loon equipment. Loon’s electrical engineering team works on everything from the circuit boards that control the system to the energy balance between the lithium ion batteries that store solar power and the panels that collect it.

Mechanical engineer Dan, and Operations Manager TJ celebrate after the first successful test of the prototype system they designed to improve the process of launching balloons.

The free space optical communications team is working on technology to enable high bandwidth communications links for Loon. Here, the team takes a fun break to capture a laser beam pattern on film using dry ice (see the next picture for the result).

The team’s creative masterpiece: a red and green pattern, created using laser beams in Loon’s free-space optical communications lab.

Join us

The Project Loon team is building a network of balloons that fly around the globe on the stratospheric winds and provide Internet access to everyone below. We’re searching for talented and inspired engineers and scientists to join us.
Our teams execute quickly and test prototype solutions with limited guidance or structure. We thrive on stimulating,
fast-paced, and inventive work and are passionate about engineering excellence.

Qualified candidates will have significant prototyping experience and a strong analytical foundation
(MS/PhD or equivalent) in these fields:

  • Firmware and controls
  • Free space optical communication
  • Electrical engineering
  • Mechanical engineering

Interested? Please submit your resume to one of our open roles.
